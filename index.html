Climate Change

Transport and burning fossil fuels are harming the environment.

These emissions are composed of greenhouse gases like CO2 and Methane.
They are released into the earth's atmosphere which makes it trap more heat.

A warmer Earth causes ice caps to melt.
Animals who depend on it for survival such as polar bears will perish.

Weather patterns will change so severe weather will happen more frequently.

Droughts and floods can destroy crops.


Species that have become extinct due to Climate Change

- More than 1 million species face extinction right now

Monarch Butterfly

As the temperature increases, their range becomes drier resulting in loss of vital food plants.

Pika

Close relatives of rabbits and hares, Pikas are adapted to cold areas. They will have nowhere to go because their are very intolerant of high temperatures.

Leatherback Sea Turtle

Pollution, rising sea levels, and extreme weather events washes away their nests, decreasing their population.

Koala

They can only eat eucalyptus leaves but due to the increasing level of CO2, the nutritional value of leaves will decrease, causing the koalas to starve.

Polar Bear

As the glaciers melt, they have no place to find prey like seals. So they are forced to forage on land and may come into conflict with humans.

FAQs About Climate Change and The Earth

What caused climate change?

We did. Human activities are releasing huge amounts of greenhouse gases into the atmosphere, increasing the earth's temperature, leading to global warming.

How much ice does the earth lose every year?

About 1.2 trillion tons.

How often do sea levels rise?

About 3.6mm per year.

Until what time will climate change be irreversible?

2030. Only about 10 years time.
